About Me

I am a graduate research assistant in Electrical Transmission and Distribution Networks Laboratory (ETD Lab.) at Tarbiat Modares University, where I am fortunate to be supervised by Prof. Mahmoud-Reza Haghifam. I received my B.Sc. and M.Sc. degrees in Electrical Engineering from Babol Noshirvani University of Technology and Tarbiat Modares University, respectively.

Research Interests

I have a broad interest in theoretical computer science and economics. My main interests lie at the intersection of Differential Privacy, Algorithmic Game Theory, Trustworthy Machine Learning, and Bayesian Inference. In particular, I have strong interests in uncertainty quantification and robustness in machine learning models with an emphasis on tractable, nonparametric, and probabilistic learning. My primary focus is to study the new algorithmic, economic, and societal challenges in socio-technical systems, in particular, future power and energy systems.
