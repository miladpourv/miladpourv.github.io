About Me

I am a graduate research assistant at Tarbiat Modares University, where I am fortunate to be supervised by Prof. Mahmoud-Reza Haghifam. I received my B.Sc. and M.Sc. degrees in Electrical Engineering from Babol Noshirvani University of Technology and Tarbiat Modares University, respectively.

Research Interests

My research lies broadly at the intersection of Theoretical Computer Science and Economics. Specifically, I am interested in Differential Privacy, Algorithmic Fairness, Algorithmic Game Theory, Learning Theory, and their intersections. My research goal is to address the emerging algorithmic challenges in socio-technical systems in the presence of societal and economic considerations, e.g., privacy, fairness, and efficiency. In particular, I have a strong interest in data markets and data acquisition mechanisms within a societal context, where the goal is to incentivize privacy-aware and strategic agents to share their data and behave truthfully.

Feel free to email me if you come across any research interests we may have in common. I am always open to potential collaborations.
